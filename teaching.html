Teaching

I contribute to teaching within the Department of Geoscience and Remote Sensing at TU Delft.

ECTM3040 – Atmospheric Physics for Renewable Energy

This course explores the atmospheric processes that shape wind and solar energy resources. Students gain an understanding of atmospheric boundary-layer dynamics, turbulence, surface–atmosphere exchange, and radiative transfer, and how these processes influence renewable energy generation. Through the analysis of atmospheric observations and numerical weather model simulations, students develop practical skills in assessing atmospheric conditions relevant to wind and solar energy systems.

ECTB2240 – Modeling and Simulation

This course introduces the mathematical and numerical methods used to model Earth system processes, including groundwater flow, ocean circulation, atmospheric dynamics, and wave propagation. Students learn how governing equations are formulated, discretized in space and time, and implemented in numerical simulations. The course covers numerical integration, interpolation, discretization techniques for partial differential equations, optimization, and high-performance computing, providing the foundations for scientific modelling across the geosciences.
