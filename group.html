Sara Porchetta

Principal Investigator

I joined TU Delft as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Geoscience and Remote Sensing in February 2024. I completed my Ph.D. on the effects of air–sea interaction on offshore wind energy in 2021 at KU Leuven, in collaboration with the von Karman Institute for Fluid Dynamics.

Following my Ph.D., I worked at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology on online coupled modelling of energy-system expansion within numerical weather prediction models.

Gabriel Barbieri Doumont

PhD Student

Gabriel is a PhD researcher investigating how offshore wind farms influence sea surface temperature through atmosphere–ocean interactions. His research combines observations with coupled atmosphere–wave–ocean simulations to better understand the physical feedbacks between offshore wind farms and the marine environment.

Menno Veerman

Postdoctoral Researcher

Menno is a postdoctoral researcher working on the interaction between wind farms and precipitation. Large wind farms may affect precipitation patterns in their vicinity due to upstream convergence and downstream divergence. However, observational and modelling evidence for this mechanism is still very limited.

His research aims to better understand where, when, and why offshore wind farms influence precipitation by combining observational precipitation products with high-resolution simulations.
